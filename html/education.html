Selected work

Here are some of my projects from undergraduate years and more. Computer graphics projects were created with Zack Mayeda.

Bezier Surfaces

Developed a program that does the following:

  • tessellates Bezier surfaces into triangles.
  • computes the exact Gaussian Curvature of the surface directly from the Weingarten map.
  • computes an approximation to the Gaussian Curvature based only on the triangles and normal vectors at the triangle vertices.

Below are the results we achieved. The images on the left were rendererd according to the exact Gaussian Curvature, and the images on the right were renderered with the approximate Gaussian Curvature. The images are generally consistent except for the tearing on the surface of the approximations.

AI/Machine Learning/Computation Biology

I worked on several projects relevant to AI/ML/Computaional Biology.

Neural Networks

Implementated backpropagation algorithm for training multilayer neural networks. Trained several neural networks on the MNIST dataset and evaluated results.

I also experimented with new features, in addition to using the standard pixel features. The additional features attempt to capture how many "holes" each digit has by counting the number of times a group of pixels in a row or column change from on to off.

Hidden Markov Models

Implemented Expecation-Maximization algorithm to estimate the parameters of a Hidden Markov Model. Used the model to predict the time of most recent common ancestor between two DNA sequences at each position along the sequence.

Pacman AI

Implemented AI algorithms for playing Pacman. This included Expectimax, Q-Learning, Particle Filtering, and more.

Placed 5th out of approximately 80 teams in class Pacman contest. Our bot used primarily alpha-beta pruning, as well as particle filtering for tracking the enemy's location.
